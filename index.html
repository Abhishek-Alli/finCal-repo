SIP Calculator

Calculate your potential returns from Systematic Investment Plans

What is SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is an investment strategy that allows you to invest a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds. Instead of investing a lump sum amount, you invest smaller amounts at regular intervals (monthly, quarterly, etc.).

Benefits of SIP

  • Disciplined Investing: SIP instills financial discipline by making you invest regularly.
  • Rupee Cost Averaging: You buy more units when prices are low and fewer units when prices are high.
  • Power of Compounding: Your returns generate more returns over time.
  • Flexibility: You can start, stop, or modify your SIP anytime.
  • Affordability: You can start with as low as ₹500 per month.

How to Use This Calculator

  2. Set your expected annual return rate
  3. Choose your investment period in years
  4. View your estimated returns and total value

SWP Calculator

Calculate your systematic withdrawals from your investment corpus

What is SWP?

A Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) is a facility that allows you to withdraw a fixed amount from your mutual fund investments at regular intervals. It's the reverse of a SIP and is typically used during retirement to generate a regular income.

Benefits of SWP

  • Regular Income: Provides a steady cash flow to meet your expenses.
  • Tax Efficiency: Only the gains portion of your withdrawal is taxable.
  • Flexibility: You can choose the withdrawal amount and frequency.
  • Capital Appreciation: Your remaining corpus continues to grow.
  • Financial Independence: Helps maintain your lifestyle without depleting your corpus quickly.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter your initial investment amount
  2. Set your expected annual return rate
  3. Choose your withdrawal amount and period
  4. View your total withdrawals and remaining corpus

EMI Calculator

Calculate your Equated Monthly Installments for various loans

What is EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. EMIs are used to pay off both interest and principal each month, so that over a specified number of years, the loan is paid off in full.

EMI Calculation Formula

The formula to calculate EMI is:

EMI = [P x R x (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N-1]

Where:

  • P = Principal loan amount
  • R = Monthly interest rate (annual rate/12/100)
  • N = Loan tenure in months

Factors Affecting EMI

  • Loan Amount: Higher loan amount means higher EMI
  • Interest Rate: Higher interest rate increases EMI
  • Loan Tenure: Longer tenure reduces EMI but increases total interest
  • Down Payment: Higher down payment reduces loan amount and EMI

Retirement Planner

Plan your retirement corpus and monthly income needs

Retirement Planning

Retirement planning is the process of determining retirement income goals and the actions and decisions necessary to achieve those goals. It includes identifying sources of income, estimating expenses, implementing a savings program, and managing assets.

Key Retirement Planning Steps

  1. Set Retirement Goals: Determine when you want to retire and what lifestyle you want.
  2. Calculate Retirement Corpus: Estimate how much money you'll need for retirement.
  3. Assess Current Savings: Evaluate your current retirement savings and investments.
  4. Create Savings Plan: Determine how much you need to save regularly to reach your goal.
  5. Investment Strategy: Choose appropriate investment vehicles for your retirement corpus.
  6. Regular Review: Periodically review and adjust your retirement plan.
